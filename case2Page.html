Designstudio - Summary

Jump too case study

Brief / Problem space

Due to unclarities during the initial brief explanation the project group and the representative from Region halland bemaningsenheten drafted a new brief.

The project leader and the representative from Region halland were quick to design a new brief with a different problem space that our group could work on. It was only later that we were informed about the miscommunication.

The new briefs problem space encompassed Region halland and the bemanningsenheten. Today region hallands bemanningsenheten is in need of a more effective system for booking personnel who can drive and administer proper care inside of ambulances. This includes dividing work hours more efficiently and equally, seeing what competences different individuals have as well as having an efficient overview of work hours that are missing personnel and what competencies are needed inside the ambulance.

Goals

Due to the systems used by Region halland being highly confidential, the project team aimed to gain a greater understanding of how the booking system works today, identifying areas of improvement and creating a more effective an user friendly artefact for both the administrators and personnel.

Results

The project resulted in a thought out artefact more catered towards the different users of the system. The artefact aimed to streamline booking done by the administrators of the system as well as giving personnel a better overview, and more security in their work hours. With this said, a design manual was developed, but further improvements and evaluations of the artefact needs to be carried out before its put to use inside of such a sensitive area.

However the representatives were very happy with the prototype that were developed for them in such a short timespan. And that it gave them a lot of insights into how a possible booking system could work in the future.

The final solution consisted of:

• A Sitemap

• A mixed fidelity prototype in figma

• A design manual

From this project I learned the importance of using off the shelf code to be able to finish and understand how to code complicated functions. Research

After establishing the brief and, with it the need for a proper booking system for Bemmaningsenheten we then started doing research with different design activities. We started off with a competitor analysis, where we studied different booking systems used amongst different companies.

The evaluation of these booking methods were graded on a point scale were 1 was the least user friendly, and 5 the most user friendly. The five categories that were graded was, simpleness of use, time to book different timeslots, cost of the booking medium and availability for actors. The project team garnered insights regarding how the future booking artefact should preferably function. Where some of the most important insights the booking system being easy to learn, being limited to a specific app where all the information was stored, this to avoid notifications being lost amidst other notifications e.g. amongst users private mails, and that cost did not play a major role to most companies due to it being relatively small to a big, well funded company.

Interviews were also conducted with administrative and on the ground personnel to garner insights into how the system works today. After this the project team used cooperativ design with most of the interviewees to create a lifelike persona that could guide the future design.

Five categories of UX - Compeeting solutions

Simpleness of use - How easy is the artefact to get used to and understand?

Ease of booking different timeslots - How does the solution make sure booking timeslots is efficient and user friendly?

Cost of the booking medium - Is the booking medium competitivley priced for the experience its offering?

Availability and flexibility for actors - Is the availability and flexibility of the solution satisfactory for actors within Region Halland - Ambulansbemmaningen?

Ideation

The project team started of the ideation phase by co-designing a persona with individuals from Region Halland who would use the system. This was done at the end of each conducted interview.

With the persona as a base, a scenario could be developed to further increase understanding, and empathize with the users to be.

Next up the project team started creating a sitemap based on the analysed interviews.

Once the sitemap was completed the project team created four different wireframes that were created on paper and in the wireframing tool balsamiq. These different wireframes and their elements were discussed and Frankensteined together based on their strengths and weaknesses.

Prototype & Evaluation

The project team then started creating a high fidelity prototype in figma. The prototype in figma showed a fully functioning flow chart of the navigational architecture, and a booking system. This was meant as a highly detailed example of how a future implemented artefact would look and could work in practice.

Besides the figma prototype a coded prototype was created by me, with input and help from another group member. The prototype was very mixed fidelity and a very early work. Initially I expected it'd be easy to create a calendar, but I ran into problems when trying to make it dynamic. A variety of different prebuilt calendars were used in the prototype before settling on an adequate one. A coded prototype was created solely for the purpose of showing that it was technically possible to create a booking system, and was far from as complete as the figma prototype, and only a proof of concept.

The project team created an evaluation guide for the figma prototype. The evaluation guide was based on tasks that were measured in completion time, with follow up questions regarding the navigational and informational architecture off the prototype.

All in all four evaluations were conducted, two with non expert users and two with expert users of the current system. During the interviews small insights were gained into little things that could be tweaked to make the artefact even better.

This was probably due to the low sample size of the evaluation and most of the large issues with the prototype being hammered out in the early stages of development through critic sessions held with other students during the design studio.

Through the earlier stages of development the artefact garnered criticism towards different areas, mostly directed towards how information was presented and how it could be presented more clearly. E.g. what region a work pass was related to and the size and readability of buttons.

Final artefact

With major flaws in the artefact being corrected for during the earlier stages of development the final artefact resulted in having a more refined menu structure, a revised and clearer schedule structure, increasing the size of certain buttons (to make them easier to press) and changing different markings in the schedule.

As an example of this, the marker for todays date was under heavy debate throughout the process. Although this was most likely due to different schedule application marking the date differently. And the project team finally settled on a marking that was deemed adequate for most users.
